# Why We Bought ServiceNow

**Memo · 2026-04-17 · 4 shares at $75 GTC limit · Entry pending Monday**

---

In April of this year, we took a starter position in ServiceNow at seventy-five dollars — a price forty-six percent below its fifty-two-week high [SRC: S1]. What attracted us was not the story the Street is telling about this company, but the one it isn't.

Every platform-software name this cycle has been punished for the same crime. The market believes agentic AI will disintermediate the workflow layer entirely. Microsoft Copilot will eat Salesforce. Google Gemini will eat Adobe. Agentic ChatGPT will eat ServiceNow. The argument is neat. It sells clicks. It has also priced a very high-quality franchise as if its moat had already evaporated — and the math, once you sit with it, does not cooperate with that view.

In its last fiscal year, ServiceNow produced $4.58 billion of free cash flow, growing 34 percent [SRC: S1]. Its data-center subscription revenue hit $5.38 billion in a single quarter, up 39 percent year over year. Its renewal rate sits above 98 percent — a number that does not belong to a disintermediated business. Applying a conservative 8 percent discount rate to current cash flows, earnings-power-value sits near $180 per share. Even stripping out thirty percent for stock-based compensation, you are left with an intrinsic value north of $130. The stock is at $83.

What moved us from "interesting" to "actionable" was that Klarman — normally our most skeptical voice, and a legend who almost never calls a franchise growth name a bull — turned bull at these levels [SRC: committee rumble, 2026-04-17]. When the margin-of-safety analyst agrees with the innovation analyst, you are looking at a narrative that has *overshot*, not a thesis that has *broken*. Howard Marks' pendulum sits near fear — but analyst consensus is still 30 Buy, 4 Hold, 1 Sell. That gap between tape and sentiment is the setup.

We honor the bear case. The trend-follower in our committee is short. The fifty-day moving average sits below the two-hundred-day, and that death cross is not a nothing. If Microsoft lands a major enterprise deal that displaces ServiceNow workflows — or if Now Assist's $1B annual contract value target fails to track by mid-year — or if any hyperscaler cuts their 2026 AI capex guide by more than ten percent — the thesis is invalidated. We will exit. We documented these triggers before we bought.

The position is four shares. Three hundred dollars. Roughly four percent of the account. Stop at seventy-two — below the fifty-two-week-low support and roughly ten percent below our entry. Maximum loss on this trade is forty-four dollars. Our first target is the two-hundred-day moving average reclaim at $110; our second, the consensus analyst target at $182. Our time horizon is twelve months. We reassess at thirty, sixty, and ninety days.

We are not trying to call the bottom. We are trying to buy quality at a price where being wrong is cheap and being right is cleanly structured. That is the entire job.

*If the crowd is already bearish and the analyst is still bullish, the crowd usually catches up.*
